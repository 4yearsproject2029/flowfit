# Product Brief

## Vision

RepLog turns fitness consistency into a game.

The product helps beginner and returning fitness users build sustainable workout habits by making each completed workout feel rewarding, visible, and worth coming back for. RepLog is not designed to create elite athletes. It is designed to help people become consistent exercisers.

Users should feel proud that they showed up, even before they see major physical results.

## Problem Statement

Many workout apps focus on performance tracking, detailed analytics, public comparison, and advanced training optimization. These products can work well for experienced fitness users, but they often create friction and pressure for beginners.

Beginner users may stop exercising because motivation fades, routines break after missed days, logging feels like work, and progress feels too small or embarrassing to share. RepLog solves this by shifting the product experience from "How strong are you?" to "How consistently are you showing up?"

## Target Users

### Primary Users

Beginner fitness users who started working out recently and want motivation more than analytics.

They typically:

- Exercise 1 to 4 times per week.
- Want to build a sustainable workout habit.
- Feel intimidated by traditional fitness apps.
- Enjoy game-like progress systems.
- Use social platforms and may want aesthetic, low-pressure ways to share progress.
- Value encouragement, simplicity, and self-expression.

### Secondary Users

Returning and intermediate fitness users who already exercise or have exercised before but want a lighter, more motivating way to stay accountable.

They may already use workout trackers, notes, spreadsheets, or social posts, but want a product that makes consistency feel more rewarding without pushing them into advanced analytics or comparison.

## User Pain Points

- Existing workout apps feel too complicated, technical, or serious.
- Users lose motivation after missing a few workouts.
- Recording workouts can feel like extra work instead of support.
- Progress feels invisible when physical results are slow.
- Users feel embarrassed to share low weights, reps, or beginner-level numbers.
- Social fitness products can create comparison pressure.
- Users want encouragement without being judged, ranked, or publicly evaluated.

## Core Value Proposition

RepLog helps users build workout consistency by turning completed workouts into simple rewards: XP, levels, badges, streaks, weekly goals, and shareable achievement cards.

Unlike performance-heavy fitness apps, RepLog celebrates showing up rather than lifting more, ranking higher, or optimizing every metric. It gives users a fast way to log workouts, a clear sense of progress, and private-by-default sharing moments that make small wins feel meaningful.

## Success Metrics

- Weekly active users.
- Average workouts logged per user per week.
- Weekly goal completion rate.
- Consecutive weekly goal completion rate.
- 30-day retention rate.
- Average share cards generated per user per week.
- Percentage of users completing a first workout after onboarding.
- Percentage of users who return after missing a workout week.

## MVP Scope

### Must Have

- Fast workout logging focused on completion and consistency.
- User-defined weekly workout goal.
- Workout completion rewards.
- XP system that rewards showing up, weekly goals, and healthy consistency.
- Level progression that communicates habit growth rather than athletic superiority.
- Clear progress visibility, including current level, XP, and progress toward the next level.
- Basic streak or consistency tracking that allows healthy rest and does not punish missed days harshly.
- Share cards for workout completion, level-up moments, and weekly goal completion.
- Private-by-default experience with optional sharing.
- Beginner-friendly product language that celebrates small wins.
- Offline-first experience with local storage.
- MVP platform scope limited to iOS.

### Should Have

- Badge unlocks for major consistency milestones.
- Encouraging celebration moments after workout completion and level-up.
- Weekly progress summary.
- Simple customization for share cards, such as theme or visible metrics.
- Minimal outcome tracking for workout completion, XP earned, share card creation, and weekly goal completion.
- Accessibility support through clear typography, large touch targets, high contrast, and predictable navigation.

### Could Have

- Monthly recap share cards.
- More share card templates and seasonal themes.
- Cosmetic level rewards such as badge frames, profile icons, or card themes.
- Returning-user encouragement after missed weeks.
- Custom badges.
- Premium cosmetic themes.
- Android or web support after MVP validation.
- Cloud backup or multi-device support.
- Apple Watch integration.
- Seasonal challenges.

### Won't Have

- Public rankings.
- Leaderboards.
- Social feed.
- Friend system.
- Messaging.
- In-app comments, likes, or followers.
- Professional coaching tools.
- Advanced analytics dashboards.
- AI coaching or AI workout suggestions.
- Nutrition tracking.
- Video workout content.
- Wearable integrations for MVP.
- Desktop support.
- Authentication for MVP.
- Cloud synchronization for MVP.
- Pay-to-win mechanics, XP multipliers, or performance advantages.

## Risks

- Gamification could feel shallow if rewards do not create meaningful motivation.
- Too many progression systems could make the MVP feel complex instead of simple.
- Share cards may not create value if users still feel embarrassed by fitness progress.
- XP rules may be confusing unless users understand why rewards were earned.
- Streak mechanics could create guilt if they punish missed workouts too strongly.
- Beginner focus may limit appeal for advanced users, which is acceptable for MVP but should remain intentional.
- The product may drift toward analytics or social comparison if scope is not actively managed.
- Users may lose their data if they uninstall the app or switch devices. This trade-off is intentionally accepted during the MVP phase to reduce onboarding friction, eliminate backend complexity, and accelerate product validation. Future versions may introduce optional cloud backup and synchronization.

## Open Questions

- What is the minimum workout information required for users to feel they logged enough?
- Should missed weeks reset consecutive weekly milestones or simply pause momentum?
- Which badge types are essential for MVP, if any?
- Should level-up celebrations be skippable?
- How much share card customization is necessary for users to feel comfortable posting?
- Should users be able to hide all performance numbers by default?
- What weekly goal options should be offered at launch?
- What onboarding question best identifies a user's desired weekly workout target?
