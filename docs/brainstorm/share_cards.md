# RepLog Share Cards

## Purpose

Share cards transform private progress into shareable moments.

The goal is not to show how strong users are.

The goal is to celebrate consistency.

Share cards help users think:

"I want to share that I showed up today."

instead of:

"I need impressive numbers to post."

---

## Design Principles

Share cards should feel:

* Encouraging
* Aesthetic
* Low pressure
* Personal
* Celebratory

Share cards should avoid:

* Comparison
* Competition
* Embarrassment
* Information overload

---

## Core Philosophy

Traditional fitness sharing focuses on performance.

RepLog focuses on participation.

Users should feel comfortable sharing progress regardless of fitness level.

The card should communicate:

* Effort
* Consistency
* Identity

Not:

* Strength
* Rank
* Status

---

## Default Information

Prioritize:

* Current level
* XP progress
* Streak count
* Badge earned
* Workout completion
* Weekly goal progress

Optional:

* Workout duration
* Workout category
* Mood

Hidden by default:

* Weight lifted
* Sets and reps
* Personal records
* Body weight
* Calories burned

Users may choose to reveal these later.

## Personalization

Users can customize:

* Themes
* Background colors
* Card layouts
* Avatar
* Displayed metrics

Future possibilities:

* Seasonal themes
* Limited event themes

---

## Privacy Principles

Sharing is optional.

Users control:

* What to share
* When to share
* Which metrics are visible

Default behavior:

Private first.

---

## Virality Goals

When others see a share card, they should think:

"What app is that?"

Share cards should naturally encourage curiosity.

Success indicators:

* Share card creation rate
* Story repost rate
* New installs from referrals
