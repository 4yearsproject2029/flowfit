# RepLog Target Users

## Primary Users

### Beginner Fitness Users ("헬린이")

People who:

* Started working out within the last 12 months
* Struggle to maintain consistency
* Feel intimidated by traditional fitness apps
* Want motivation more than analytics
* Enjoy game-like experiences

Typical characteristics:

* Age: 20–39
* Exercise frequency: 1–4 times per week
* Uses Instagram regularly
* Shares personal achievements online
* Values aesthetics and self-expression

---

## User Goals

Primary users want to:

* Build a sustainable workout habit
* Stay motivated over time
* Feel proud of small wins
* Track progress without pressure
* Share achievements comfortably

Users are not trying to optimize performance.

Users are trying to stay consistent.

---

## User Pain Points

Current fitness apps often feel:

* Too complicated
* Too focused on numbers
* Too serious
* Too competitive

Common frustrations:

* "I missed a few days, so I lost motivation."
* "My workout numbers are embarrassing."
* "I don't know if I'm making progress."
* "Recording workouts feels like work."
* "I want to share my progress, but not my weak numbers."

---

## Emotional Needs

Users want to feel:

* Motivated
* Rewarded
* Encouraged
* Proud
* Consistent

Users do not want to feel:

* Judged
* Compared
* Overwhelmed
* Embarrassed

---

## Secondary Users

### Intermediate Fitness Users

People who:

* Exercise regularly
* Already use workout tracking apps
* Enjoy collecting statistics
* Want lightweight gamification

These users are important but are not the primary focus of the MVP.

---

## Non-Target Users

RepLog is not designed for:

* Professional athletes
* Personal trainers
* Bodybuilding competitors
* Powerlifters focused on performance metrics
* Users seeking advanced analytics

These users may use RepLog, but the product will not optimize for their needs.

---

## Existing Alternatives

Users currently use:

* Notes apps
* Spreadsheets
* Instagram stories
* Traditional workout tracking apps

Common alternatives include:

* Strong
* Hevy
* Boostcamp
* Fitbod

---

## Why Users Choose RepLog

Users choose RepLog because it offers:

* Simple workout logging
* Gamified progress
* Low-pressure sharing
* Aesthetic share cards
* Positive reinforcement

RepLog helps users celebrate consistency rather than performance.

---

## User Personas

### Persona 1: "Consistent Chloe"

Age:

29

Occupation:

Office worker

Goals:

* Exercise three times per week
* Build a long-term habit
* Share progress with friends

Frustrations:

* Loses motivation easily
* Feels embarrassed by workout numbers
* Finds existing apps overwhelming

Quote:

"I want to feel proud that I showed up."

---

### Persona 2: "Returning Ryan"

Age:

34

Occupation:

Engineer

Goals:

* Restart workouts after a long break
* Stay accountable
* Rebuild confidence

Frustrations:

* Breaks routines after missing workouts
* Compares current performance to the past

Quote:

"I need something that makes consistency fun again."

---

## Design Implications

RepLog should prioritize:

* Positive feedback
* Low cognitive load
* Fast logging experience
* Encouraging language
* Shareable moments

RepLog should avoid:

* Leaderboards
* Public rankings
* Aggressive competition
* Complex analytics
* Information overload

---

## Success Criteria

Users should feel:

"I worked out today."

instead of:

"I'm still not strong enough."

The product succeeds when users celebrate showing up, not outperforming others.
