# RepLog Core Values

## Our Philosophy

RepLog exists to help people build sustainable fitness habits.

We believe consistency matters more than intensity.

Users should feel rewarded for showing up, not punished for missing a day.

---

## Core Values

### 1. Consistency Over Perfection

Missing one workout does not mean failure.

The goal is long-term habit formation.

RepLog rewards users for returning, not for being perfect.

Questions we ask:

* Does this feature encourage consistency?
* Does this reduce guilt after missing workouts?

---

### 2. Progress Over Performance

Users should compare themselves to their past selves.

Not to others.

RepLog celebrates:

* Completed workouts
* Streaks
* Milestones
* Personal growth

RepLog avoids:

* Public rankings
* Leaderboards
* Competitive pressure

---

### 3. Motivation Over Analytics

Data is valuable.

Motivation is essential.

Analytics should support motivation, not overwhelm users.

We prioritize:

* Clear feedback
* Simple progress indicators
* Encouraging milestones

We avoid:

* Complex dashboards
* Excessive statistics
* Information overload

---

### 4. Fun Over Friction

Logging workouts should feel effortless.

Users should spend more time exercising than recording.

Every interaction should be:

* Fast
* Intuitive
* Rewarding

We minimize:

* Manual input
* Complex setup
* Unnecessary screens

---

### 5. Celebrate Small Wins

Every completed workout matters.

RepLog reinforces progress through:

* XP rewards
* Level progression
* Badges
* Share cards
* Positive messages

Small wins create long-term habits.

---

### 6. Private by Default

Users should control what they share.

Sharing is optional.

RepLog encourages:

* Low-pressure sharing
* Curated share cards
* User-controlled visibility

RepLog avoids:

* Forced social features
* Public profiles
* Comparison culture

---

## Product Decision Framework

Before building any feature, ask:

1. Does it help users stay consistent?
2. Does it reduce friction?
3. Does it encourage positive emotions?
4. Does it avoid unhealthy comparison?
5. Does it create shareable moments?

If the answer is "no" to most questions:

Do not build it.

---

## What RepLog Is

* A habit-building tool
* A personal fitness journal
* A gamified workout companion
* A source of positive reinforcement

---

## What RepLog Is Not

* A social network
* A bodybuilding app
* A professional coaching platform
* A competition platform
* An advanced analytics tool

---

## Success Definition

RepLog succeeds when users think:

"I want to keep my streak alive."

instead of:

"I need to beat someone else."

The goal is not stronger athletes.

The goal is more consistent exercisers.
