# RepLog Level System

## Design Principles

Levels represent consistency, not athletic ability.

Users level up by maintaining healthy workout habits.

Levels should communicate:

* Commitment
* Progress
* Identity

Levels should not communicate:

* Strength
* Fitness superiority
* Social status

---

## Core Philosophy

RepLog is a personal journey.

Users compete only with their past selves.

The level system exists to answer one question:

"How consistently have I shown up?"

---

## Progression Model

RepLog uses permanent progression.

There are:

* No seasons
* No level resets
* No ranking systems

Users keep their progress forever.

---

## Level Structure

Maximum Level:

```text id="wwr83x"
Level 50
```

Design goal:

A typical user should reach max level within approximately six months.

Assumptions:

* Weekly goal: 5 workouts
* Occasional missed weeks
* Average XP gain: 400–500 XP per week

Estimated time to max level:

```text id="ntaxw5"
24–28 weeks
```

---

## XP Curve

Required XP increases gradually.

Formula:

```text id="stl80z"
Required XP = 100 × Level^1.5
```

Goals:

* Fast early progression
* Meaningful long-term growth
* Consistent sense of achievement

## Level-Up Experience

Users should immediately feel rewarded.

Level-up feedback may include:

* Animation
* Haptic feedback
* Celebration screen
* Badge unlock
* Encouraging message

Example messages:

* "You showed up again."
* "Small wins create big changes."
* "Consistency is your superpower."

---

## XP Visibility

Users should always see:

* Current level
* Current XP
* XP required for next level
* Progress bar

Users should never feel confused about progression.

---

## Level Rewards

Level rewards should remain cosmetic.

Examples:

* New badge frames
* New share card themes
* New profile icons
* Celebration animations

Avoid:

* Gameplay advantages
* Pay-to-win mechanics
* XP multipliers

---

## Max Level Experience

Reaching max level is a celebration, not the end.

After Level 50:

* Users continue earning XP
* Additional XP contributes to lifetime statistics
* Users unlock prestige badges

Level remains:

```text id="nb1xwy"
Level 50
```

Potential future features:

* Prestige system
* Seasonal cosmetic rewards
* Legacy achievements

These features are out of scope for MVP.

---

## Anti-Pressure Rules

Users should never feel punished.

Rules:

* Missing workouts does not remove XP.
* Levels never decrease.
* Titles cannot be lost.
* Rest days are encouraged.

---

## Success Criteria

Users should think:

"I'm becoming someone who exercises consistently."

instead of:

"I'm falling behind everyone else."

The level system succeeds when it reinforces identity, not competition.

---

## Open Questions

* Should level-up celebrations be skippable?
* Should users customize titles?

These questions will be revisited after MVP validation.
